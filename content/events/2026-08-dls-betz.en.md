## Abstract

The talk presents the learning-based methods and control strategies that enable autonomous vehicles to operate under extreme conditions and make robust decisions in rapidly changing environments. Through case studies from competitions such as the Abu Dhabi Autonomous Racing League, the lecture highlights how autonomous racing is pushing the boundaries of safety, reliability, and vehicle performance.

## Topics Covered

- Whether autonomous vehicles can outperform human drivers at the limits of vehicle dynamics
- Learning-based motion planning and control strategies for high-speed autonomous racing
- Robust autonomous decision-making under uncertainty and rapidly changing conditions
- Insights from real-world autonomous motorsport competitions, including the Abu Dhabi Autonomous Racing League
- How autonomous racing research advances safety, reliability, and the future of intelligent mobility
